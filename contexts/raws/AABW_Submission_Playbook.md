# Hackathon Submission Playbook

**Agentic AI Build Week 2026**  
**Location:** Ho Chi Minh City  
**Dates:** July 8-12, 2026

Do's, don'ts, and what judges are looking for at Agentic AI Build Week - so your team submits with confidence.

---

## 1. What Judges Look For

### Agentic AI Use

Planning, reasoning, tool use, workflow execution, or multi-step autonomous action - not a single prompt-response wrapper.

### Problem / Track Fit

The project clearly addresses the selected track and its real enterprise problem statement.

### Technical Execution

The project is functional, technically credible, and shows thoughtful implementation.

### Impact / Usefulness

Clear value for real users, teams, organizations, or communities.

### Creativity

Novel, differentiated, or an original approach to the problem.

### Pitch / Demo Clarity

The team communicates the problem, solution, and value clearly inside the time box.

---

## 2. Submission Best Practices - Part 1: Do This

1. **Build one project per track when possible.**  
   If your project is genuinely relevant to more than one track, you may submit it to each - but every individual submission must be assigned to exactly one track.

2. **Show planning and tool use, not just output.**  
   Agentic AI Use is what judges look for first - make your reasoning and multi-step action visible.

3. **Have a working demo, tested live.**  
   Technical execution is judged in the room - rehearse the exact flow you will show.

4. **Submit through the AABW event portal.**  
   Do not submit through Devpost. Fill in every required hackathon submission field completely.

5. **Confirm your track by July 11, 3:00 PM.**  
   No confirmation on the event portal by then means no eligibility for judging.

6. **Submit by July 12, 9:00 AM, and check in in person.**  
   At least one team representative must check in on-site to confirm your team is pitching.

---

## 3. Submission Best Practices - Part 2: Avoid This

1. **Do not enter a track that is not genuinely relevant.**  
   You can submit the same project to more than one track if it truly fits each one, but forcing an unrelated track hurts your Problem / Track Fit score.

2. **Do not wrap a single API call and call it "agentic."**  
   Judges are specifically looking for planning, reasoning, and multi-step action.

3. **Do not run over your pitch time.**  
   - Round 1: 8 minutes total
     - 5 minutes pitch
     - 2 minutes Q&A
     - 1 minute transition
   - Round 2: 12 minutes total

   Timekeeping is strict.

4. **Do not lead with your tech stack.**  
   Open with the problem and the "aha" moment, not the architecture.

5. **Do not wait until judging day to test your demo.**  
   Live failures are visible and directly hurt Technical Execution.

6. **Do not leave fields blank or unchecked.**  
   Double-check that every field in the event portal - description, track, tools used, and team information - is complete and accurate before July 12, 9:00 AM.

## 4. Frequently Asked Questions

### What makes a strong submission?

A clear one-line problem statement, a working live demo rather than just slides, and an accurate list of every AI tool, model, and partner credit you used.

### Where do I submit?

The AABW event portal. Devpost is no longer used for this event.

### Can I submit the same project to more than one track?

Yes, if it is genuinely relevant to each track. It is not recommended to enter a track your project does not truly fit.

### What is the most common submission mistake?

Leaving required fields blank or entering inaccurate information. The description, team list, tools used, and track selection all need to be complete and correct.

### Do I need to be there in person?

Yes. At least one team representative must check in on-site on July 12 to confirm your team is pitching.

### Why does it matter which tools I record?

Several technology partners run bonus prizes in addition to track prizes. Recording and correctly selecting the tools you used is what makes you eligible for those prizes.

### Can we get real data from our enterprise partner?

The organizers are pursuing it, but if it is not available by the end of the current building day, it is probably not coming. Plan to build with synthetic data instead. If your idea cannot work with synthetic data, remove that part of the idea rather than waiting for the data.

## 5. Remaining Best Practices

1. **State the problem in one sentence before the solution.**  
   Make your Problem / Track Fit unmissable from the first line of your pitch.

2. **Build for the actual enterprise brief, not a hypothetical.**  
   AABW tracks are grounded in real problem statements from partners such as AWS, Vietjet, Tasco, and KFC Vietnam. Solve the brief that was given.

3. **Record a backup demo video.**  
   Wi-Fi and API failures can happen inside your pitch window. A fallback protects your Technical Execution score.

4. **Record every tool and technology partner you use accurately.**  
   Several technology partners run bonus prizes in addition to track prizes, so select your tools correctly in the submission portal.

5. **Disclose any pre-existing code or assets.**  
   Using something you built before AABW is acceptable. Failing to disclose it is not.

6. **Assign one team member to own the pitch.**  
   A single confident narrator communicates more clearly than a group hand-off.

7. **Treat Demo Day as a pilot conversation.**  
   Enterprise partners are in the room to find teams to work with after judging, not just to hand out trophies.

## 6. Submission Portal - Steps 1-2: Team Setup and Project Overview

### Team Name

Keep it consistent with your project branding. It appears on your public profile and Demo Day materials.

### Invite Teammates

Add every teammate before the roster locks. Only the team captain can edit it afterward.

### Choose Tracks

Select only the track or tracks your project genuinely fits. Problem statements are locked to the tracks you choose here.

### Selected Problem Statement

Pick the exact problem statement your build addresses. It is the direct anchor for your Problem / Track Fit.

### Project Title

Make it descriptive and memorable, not clever but vague. It is the first thing judges and the public gallery see.

### Elevator Pitch

Write one or two plain-language sentences describing what the product does and who it is for. This is your project's first impression.

---

## 7. Submission Portal - Step 3: The Project Story Field

The **About the project** field is the field judges read most closely. Structure it using the following sections.

### 1. Inspiration

What real problem or moment sparked this build? Ground it in something specific, not generic.

### 2. What It Does

Describe the product in plain language before giving technical details.

### 3. How You Built It

Name your actual architecture and tools. This is where Technical Execution is judged.

### 4. Challenges You Ran Into

Be honest about what was difficult and how you solved it. Judges read this as a signal of depth.

### 5. Accomplishments You Are Proud Of

Choose one concrete moment or result, not a generic superlative.

### 6. What You Learned

Share a real insight, not a platitude.

### 7. What Is Next

Show that you are thinking beyond the hackathon. This supports the Impact / Usefulness criterion.

### Built With - Tags

List every tool, framework, platform, database, API, or model by its exact name. This directly influences how judges read your Agentic AI Use and Technical Execution.

---

## 8. Submission Portal - Step 3 Continued: Links, Media, and Partner Tools

### Demo URL

Test it in an incognito window immediately before submitting. A broken or access-restricted link can cost Technical Execution points during judging.

### GitHub / Repository URL

Make sure the repository is public or accessible to judges, and that the README explains how to run the project.

### Image Gallery

Upload three to five screenshots of the actual product in use.

- Recommended ratio: 3:2
- Screenshots should be readable at a glance

### Video Demo Link

Keep the video between two and three minutes and show the product working. Do not narrate over slides only.

### AABW Technology Partner Tools

Select every partner tool you genuinely used. This is what makes you eligible for that partner's bonus prizes.

### Explain Partner Tool Usage

Be specific about what each tool actually did in your build. Vague answers may risk bonus-prize eligibility.

## 9. Submission Portal - Step 4: Before You Hit Submit

1. **Official Rules checkbox - required**  
   Read the rules before checking the box. Every team member is bound by them once the project is submitted.

2. **Public visibility checkbox**  
   Check it if you want your project visible on builder profiles and in the public gallery. Leave it unchecked only if you have a specific reason not to make the project public.

3. **In-person check-in**  
   At least one teammate must physically check in on July 12. Absent teams risk disqualification even if their submission is complete.

4. **Final submission check**  
   Review the team, track, and problem-statement summary shown on this step before confirming. This is your last chance to catch a wrong track or missing field.

> **Use this as your checklist:** Every field above maps one-to-one to the actual AABW submission portal. Work through it from top to bottom as you fill out your entry.
